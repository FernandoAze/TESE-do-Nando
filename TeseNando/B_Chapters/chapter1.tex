\section{Contextualization} \label{sec:context}
% What is MIR. Visualization in MIR. 
Music Information Retrieval (MIR) is a research field that develops computational methods to analyze, interpret, and retrieve musical data from performance recordings. MIR is heavily reliant on data, and thus, visualization plays a crucial role; how and what we visualize in data can shape not only the conclusions one makes, as well as providing guidance on how to further progress. The problem of visualization in MIR is especially critical because music is inherently subjective and abstract. Music is also a uniquely auditory medium, translating it into visual form is a challenge that spans millennia, with many attempts at "musical written" languages throughout history. Yet this task remains unfolding. For MIR researchers, this means working with diverse data representations, each capturing different aspects of music.

Music is a uniquely auditory medium, translating music into the visual domain is a problem that spans millennia, throughout History multiple attempts for "musical written" languages have been developed, yet to this day this is an unfolding task. For MIR this means that data can come in various forms: from audio-visual representations, to musical scores, to even emotions and feelings (i.e. How music feels? happy, sad, angry, etc...). There for, we use different visual mediums to display different information about music. A audio-visualization of a musical performance i.e. a waveform or spectrogram, might give information on how the performance sounds, yet it can't by itself, represent how a musical piece should be performed like a musical score does. At the same time, neither audio data or musical scores can effectively capture emotions or feelings of the musical piece, to translate such ideas text based descriptions might be best. Yet, all of these mediums serve separate important rules, while a audio-visualizations might be essential for audio engineers they are not as useful for the performer that is heavily reliant on the score.
